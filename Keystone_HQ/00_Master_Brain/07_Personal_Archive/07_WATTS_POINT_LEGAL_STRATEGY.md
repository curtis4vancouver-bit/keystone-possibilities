# WATTS POINT PROPERTY: LEGAL STRATEGY & ACTION PLAN

## THE SITUATION
- Location: Watts Point, Squamish area, BC.
- Land Status: Provincial Crown Land.
- Overlapping Tenure: Lehigh Cement holds a lease (starting ~1981/1983).
- Occupancy: Family established house, foundation, septic, garden circa 1968-1969 (10-15 years BEFORE Lehigh).
- Utility Proof: BC Hydro hookup installed in 1969.
- Current Status: Crown threatened eviction ~4 years ago, then went silent.
- Total Occupation: 56+ years continuous family presence.

## YOUR LEGAL ADVANTAGES

### 1. Prior Existence Defense (10-Year Gap)
House existed ~1968. Lehigh's lease started ~1981. Crown granted lease over occupied/improved land. If Crown didn't evict in 1969-1980, they tacitly accepted presence.

### 2. The 1969 Hydro Hookup (Smoking Gun)
BC Hydro (Crown corporation) would not have installed service without permits. Installation record likely exists in archives. Proves government approved the house as habitable.

### 3. Proprietary Estoppel (Best Legal Path)
Crown threatened eviction 4 years ago then did nothing. Wayne moved back 10 years ago, improved property. Argument: "56 years of Crown acceptance. Unconscionable to evict now."

### 4. Laches Defense Against Lehigh
Lehigh aware of structure for 40+ years. Never asked you to leave. Waited too long to enforce.

## CROWN LAND OPTIONS (2026 Research)

### Option A: Residential Crown Land Lease
- BC Gov offers leases up to 30 years via FrontCounter BC.
- Rent: 2.5%-4.0% of assessed value annually.
- Warning: Squamish leaseholders saw 180-266% rent increases recently.
- Requirement: Cadastral survey required.

### Option B: Purchase Crown Land
- In limited circumstances, Crown land can be sold for residential purposes.
- Must meet Land Title Act standards. Contact CLAD directly.

### Option C: Negotiate a Carve-Out (Best Outcome)
- Crown carves out 1-2 acres around house/septic/well.
- Issues separate long-term lease or sells directly, separating from Lehigh lease.
