# FDA Peptide Regulatory Update (2026 Shift)

## Context & Regulatory Status
On April 15, 2026, the FDA removed 11 peptides from the Category 2 "Do Not Compound" list. This regulatory shift ends the blanket prohibition established in 2023, moving these compounds from an outright ban into a formal review phase (regulatory limbo).

*   **Current Status**: They are no longer banned under Category 2, but they are not yet fully cleared for legal compounding. A formal PCAC (Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee) review and subsequent rulemaking process must occur before pharmacies can compound them legally.
*   **The Hazard**: The 2023 ban forced patients to source from unregulated research chemical markets lacking sterility standards, batch testing, and quality control. Transitioning back to a regulated framework ensures purity, sterility, and accurate dosing.

## Critical Regulatory Timeline & Action Items

*   **July 9, 2026**: FDA Docket public comment period closes. 
    *   **Action**: Submit real, measurable clinical outcomes to **Docket No. FDA-2025-N6895** at regulations.gov.
*   **End of July 2026**: PCAC formal hearing for BPC-157 and TB-500.
*   **Post-July 2026**: Expected rulemaking process and public feedback period. Anticipate several months before licensed pharmacies receive a green light to compound.

1.  **Demand Verification**: If currently utilizing research-grade peptides, demand an independent third-party Certificate of Analysis (COA) from the vendor. Reject any batch scoring below **99% purity**.
2.  **Provider Integration**: Establish clinical relationships with progressive, licensed healthcare providers now so prescriptions can be fast-tracked once the compounding channel formally opens.
3.  **Core Framework Alignment**: This regulatory shift validates the clinical relevance of our core frameworks, including the growth hormone secretagogue protocols (CJC-1295 / IPAM) and the cellular-level tissue repair protocols (Wolverine Stack: BPC-157 + TB-500).
