> **DISCLAIMER:** This is an N-of-1 personal case study. Peptides are currently in regulatory gray zones and are not FDA-approved for human consumption.

### Critical Safety Protocols & Risks
1. **Gray Market Contamination (30% Rule):** Independent lab testing reveals that approximately 30% of gray market peptide samples are mislabeled, under-dosed, or contaminated. 
   * *Action:* Never rely on static PDF lab reports (which are easily doctored). Only source from suppliers utilizing verifiable, independent, third-party testing. Baseline and ongoing blood panels are mandatory to track biomarker shifts.
2. **CJC-1295 Pituitary Risk:** FDA documentation (December 2024) classified CJC-1295 as a developmental drug, citing nonclinical findings of potential DNA damage in pituitary cells. This compound must be approached with caution and distinct risk management compared to BPC-157 or TB-500.
3. **Regulatory Milestones:** BPC-157 and TB-500 were removed from the FDA Category 2 restricted list on April 22nd. Their long-term access and clinical categorization will be determined by upcoming Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) hearings.
