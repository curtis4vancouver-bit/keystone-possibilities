## The Problem: GLP-1 Muscle Wasting
Clinical data indicates a massive risk of lean tissue loss on GLP-1 receptor agonists (Semaglutide/Ozempic, Tirzepatide/Mounjaro):
* **Step 1 Trial (Semaglutide):** Average weight loss was 15.3 kg, but 6.92 kg (45%) of that was lean body mass.
* **Surmount 1 Trial (Tirzepatide):** Confirmed ~25% lean mass loss over 72 weeks.
* **Meta-Analysis (International Journal of Obesity):** Lean mass loss ranged from 25% to 40% across multiple GLP-1 medications.
* **Nuance:** UC Davis researcher Keith Baar noted some lean mass loss on DEXA scans represents beneficial liver volume shrinkage and organ fat dumping. However, significant skeletal muscle loss remains real and dangerous.
* **The Age Factor:** After age 40, sarcopenia naturally strips 3-8% of lean muscle per decade. Combining a GLP-1 with manual labor without a protocol leads to structural failure.

## The 4-Step Muscle Preservation Protocol

### Step 1: The Protein Floor (Nitrogen Balance)
* **Directive:** Maintain a strict daily baseline of protein to prevent muscle wasting under a crushed appetite.
* **Dosage:** 1.2 to 1.5 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily.
* **Wayne's Baseline:** 180 to 200 grams of pure protein daily (for a 200 lb active builder).

### Step 2: Mechanical Tension & mTOR Activation
* **Directive:** Heavy compound lifts with progressive overload to signal muscle preservation to the body.
* **Method:** Squats, deadlifts, rows, overhead presses. The last 2 reps of each set must feel close to failure.
* **Purpose:** Triggers the mTOR cellular pathway, forcing the body to burn fat instead of digesting muscle tissue for energy during a caloric deficit.

### Step 3: Intracellular Hydration (Creatine Monohydrate)
* **Directive:** 5g of creatine monohydrate daily. 
* **Protocol:** No loading phase, no cycling. Take 5g with water every morning.
* **Purpose:** Acts as an intracellular armor plate, pulling water directly into muscle cells to keep them volumized and active during restricted caloric intake.

### Step 4: Tissue Scaffolding & Recovery (GHK-Cu)
* **Directive:** Address cosmetic and structural tissue side effects (skin laxity/"Ozempic face" and hair shedding/telogen effluvium).
* **Research Compound:** GHK-Cu (Copper Peptides).
* **Mechanism:** Stimulates collagen synthesis and increases basic fibroblast growth factor by 230% to assist with tissue remodeling.
