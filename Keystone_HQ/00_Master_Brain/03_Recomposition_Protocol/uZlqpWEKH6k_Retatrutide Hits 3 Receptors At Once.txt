# Retatrutide (Triple G Agonist) Mechanism of Action

## Overview
Retatrutide is a highly potent next-generation weight-loss and metabolic-recomposition peptide utilizing a triple-agonist mechanism, targeting three distinct receptors simultaneously.

## The Triple-Agonist Mechanism ("Triple G")
* **GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1):** Suppresses appetite and slows gastric emptying.
* **GIP (Glucose-Dependent Insulinotropic Polypeptide):** Optimizes insulin secretion and metabolic efficiency.
* **Glucagon:** Increases energy expenditure (thermogenesis), driving the metabolic furnace hotter to burn more fuel even in the absence of calorie restriction.

## Comparative Analysis
* **Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy):** Single-receptor agonist (GLP-1).
* **Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound):** Dual-receptor agonist (GLP-1 + GIP).
* **Retatrutide:** Triple-receptor agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + Glucagon) — representing a complete shift from standard metabolic management to active structural demolition of fat tissue.
