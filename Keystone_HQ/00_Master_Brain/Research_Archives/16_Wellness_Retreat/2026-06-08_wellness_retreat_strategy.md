## Section 1: Algorithmic Diagnostics and the OAC Decoupling Penalty

### The Impact of Official Artist Channel (OAC) Decoupling

A critical event was the separation of "Keystone Protocols" content from the OAC, which was initially aggregated under "Keystone Recomposition". When a YouTube channel is upgraded to an OAC—often facilitated through DistroKid—the platform permanently merges subscribers from topic channels, Vevo channels, and existing personal channels into a single, verified destination. The foundational architecture of an OAC is explicitly designed by Google to be irreversible.

Creators who attempt to decouple or leave an OAC experience immediate and dramatic drops in analytics. Subscribers algorithmically merged into the OAC are generally lost to the newly separated channel, leading to a fragmented audience base and profound algorithmic confusion.

### Assessing the Odds of Success

The channel is not shadow-banned; it has been stripped of its historical algorithmic weight and must restart audience indexing from zero. The short-term reality dictates that the channel is in the platform's sandbox phase. However, the long-term odds remain highly favorable, provided the creator executes a high-velocity, consistent data input strategy to retrain the recommendation engine.
