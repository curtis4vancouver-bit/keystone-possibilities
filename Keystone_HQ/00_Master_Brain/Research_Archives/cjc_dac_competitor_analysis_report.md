## 6. Recurring User Questions & Feedback (Comments Section)

Analysis of comments across top competitor videos reveals several major friction points and anxieties that our content should directly address:

### 1. The Post-Injection "Flush"
> **User Concern**: *"I injected CJC-1295 and immediately got a massive head rush, my face turned red, and my heart started beating fast. Am I having an allergic reaction?"*
*   **Clinical Answer**: This is a known histamine/vasodilatory response caused by the rapid release of growth hormone-releasing hormone. It typically resolves within 15–20 minutes. We should explain why this happens and how to minimize it (e.g., injecting slowly or adjusting dose).

### 2. DAC vs. No-DAC Dosing and Cycles
> **User Concern**: *"I'm confused. Some sites say inject CJC-1295 once a week, others say 3 times a day. Which one is correct?"*
*   **Clinical Answer**: Once-weekly dosing applies strictly to **CJC-1295 with DAC** (due to its ~7-day half-life). Daily dosing (typically 1–3 times a day before bed or workout) applies strictly to **CJC-1295 No-DAC (Mod GRF 1-29)**.

### 3. The "Growth Hormone Bleed" & Pituitary Desensitization
> **User Concern**: *"Does CJC-1295 with DAC cause your pituitary gland to stop producing its own growth hormone? Will it cause insulin resistance?"*
*   **Clinical Answer**: Because DAC causes a continuous elevation of GH rather than natural pulses, it can lead to receptor desensitization and chronic elevations of insulin, which can trigger insulin resistance over time. Daily cycling of the No-DAC version avoids this by allowing GH levels to return to baseline between pulses.

### 4. Severe Water Retention & Bloating
> **User Concern**: *"My feet are swollen and my hands are tingling like I have carpal tunnel. Is this a normal side effect?"*
*   **Clinical Answer**: Water retention and extremity tingling (due to nerve compression from fluid retention) are classic symptoms of elevated growth hormone and IGF-1. We should discuss how to adjust dosing to alleviate these symptoms.

### 5. Reconstitution and Math Calculations
> **User Concern**: *"My vial has 5mg of peptide. How much bacteriostatic water do I add, and where do I pull to on an insulin syringe?"*
*   **Clinical Answer**: Reconstitution math is a massive barrier for beginners. We should provide a clear, step-by-step visual guide (e.g., adding 2mL of water to a 5mg vial means a 100mcg dose is 4 units on a U-100 syringe).

### 6. FDA Category 2 Bulk Drug Substance Classification
> **User Concern**: *"My doctor told me compounding pharmacies can't make CJC-1295 anymore. Why did the FDA ban it? Is it still safe?"*
*   **Clinical Answer**: The FDA placed CJC-1295 on the Category 2 bulk drug substances list, which restricts compounding pharmacies from preparing it due to safety and efficacy review processes. Users are forced to look at "research chemical" sources, raising the risk of impure products.
