### How long does a renovation take in Whistler?
A custom home renovation in Whistler typically takes between 6 to 12 months from concept to completion. However, this timeline depends entirely on the scope of the work and the time of year. A cosmetic interior remodel (kitchens, bathrooms, floors) can take 3 to 5 months. A full-scale structural renovation or addition can take 8 to 14 months. The biggest delay factor is usually the municipal permitting queue, which is why a Pre-Development Feasibility Study is crucial to fast-track approvals.

### What is the BC Step Code and how does it affect new construction?
The BC Energy Step Code is a provincial performance-based standard that requires new buildings to reach progressively higher levels of energy efficiency, ultimately aiming for net-zero energy ready construction by 2032. For new construction and major renovations in Whistler and the Sea-to-Sky corridor, this means your home must meet strict airtightness and thermal performance metrics. It affects everything from the thickness of your insulation and the quality of your windows to the integration of heat pumps and HRV (Heat Recovery Ventilation) systems.

### Building on a Mountain Slope: Geotechnical Challenges
Whistler's terrain presents unique challenges. Sloped lots require extensive retaining walls, bedrock blasting, and complex drainage systems to handle high annual rainfall and snowmelt. Attempting a renovation or new build without a Registered BC Hydro Civil Contractor can lead to massive cost overruns. At Keystone Possibilities, we conduct rigorous site prep and geotechnical engineering before construction begins, ensuring the foundation and civil servicing are flawless.

Many Whistler contractors use fixed-price contracts with hidden margins, meaning you pay a premium for their assumed risk. Keystone Possibilities operates as a fiduciary construction project manager. ### Winter Construction Rules in Whistler
Whistler has strict bylaws regarding winter construction and noise. Certain excavation and exterior framing tasks are heavily restricted or impossible during peak snow months. A successful Whistler renovation requires precision scheduling to ensure the building envelope is sealed and watertight before the snow flies, allowing interior work to continue seamlessly through the winter.
