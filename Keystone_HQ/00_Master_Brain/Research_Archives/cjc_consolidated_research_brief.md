## 🔬 Part 1: Chemical & Clinical Science

### 1. Structural Chemistry & Half-Life
* **Mod GRF 1-29 (CJC-1295 No-DAC):** Consists of the first 29 amino acids of human GHRH with four specific substitutions (**D-Ala²**, **Gln⁸**, **Ala¹⁵**, **Leu²⁷**) to prevent dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-IV) enzymatic breakdown and oxidation. Plasma half-life is approximately **30 minutes**.
* **CJC-1295 with DAC:** Adds a C-terminal **Lysine (Lys³⁰)** conjugated to a **Maleimidopropionic Acid (MPA)** linker ($N^\epsilon$-3-maleimidopropionyl-lysine³⁰). 
* **The Albumin Shield:** Once injected, the MPA linker covalently binds to the free thiol on **Cysteine-34 (Cys³⁴)** of circulating human serum albumin. This shields the peptide from kidney filtration and enzymatic breakdown, extending the half-life to **6–8 days**.

### 2. The Landmark 2006 Ionescu JCEM Study (PMID: 17018654)
* **Preservation of Pulsatility:** Proved that continuous stimulation by CJC-DAC does **not** flatten the natural pulsatile release of Growth Hormone (GH). Somatotropes remain responsive due to the hypothalamic **Somatostatin (SST)** feedback loop (somatostatin acts as a periodic brake that resets the receptors).
* **The GH Bleed:** Although pulses remain, the baseline valleys (troughs) between pulses never return to zero. Basal GH levels increase **approximately 7.5-fold**, causing chronic receptor occupancy.
* **IGF-1 Increase:** Mean plasma IGF-1 levels increase **1.5- to 3-fold**, with elevations persisting for 9–11 days after a single dose.
* **Long-Term Risks:** Chronic elevation of GH and IGF-1 (without rest periods) can lead to insulin resistance, fluid retention (extremity tingling/carpal tunnel), and cardiovascular risks (ventricular wall thickening / cardiomegaly).

### 3. Clinical Trial Halts
* **Phase 2 Trial (NCT00267527):** Evaluating CJC-1295 with DAC in HIV-associated visceral obesity was **terminated in July 2006 following a participant death from myocardial infarction** two hours after receiving their 11th weekly dose. This permanently halted commercial development, leaving a complete lack of long-term human safety data.

## ⚖️ Part 2: FDA & Compounding Regulatory Updates (2025–2026)

### 1. The December 4, 2024 PCAC Vote
* The FDA's Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) voted **unanimously 12-0 (0 "Yes" to 12 "No")** against including CJC-1295 (both acetate and DAC formulations) on the 503A Bulks List.
* **Key Objections:** Lack of clinical efficacy data, systemic vasodilatory side effects (hypotension, flushing, tachycardia), impurity risks, and the safety void from the halted 2006 Phase 2 trial.

### 2. The April 2026 Category 2 Reclassification
* **The Action:** The FDA removed CJC-1295 (and 11 other peptides like BPC-157 and TB-500) from the Category 2 restricted list.
* **The Reason:** This was triggered strictly because the nominators **withdrew the compounding nominations**, not because the FDA cleared the safety profile.
* **The Compounding Gray Zone:** Under Section 503A, a substance is only compoundable if it is a component of an approved drug, has a USP/NF monograph, or is on the 503A Bulks List. CJC-1295 meets none of these. Because the nomination was withdrawn, it is no longer in Category 1 (under active evaluation), meaning **there is no legal path under Section 503A for pharmacies to compound CJC-1295.**
