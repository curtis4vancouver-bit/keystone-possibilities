### What is Bill 44 and how does it affect building in BC?
Bill 44 (the SSMUH legislation) is a provincial mandate in British Columbia that fundamentally changes local zoning laws. It requires municipalities, including those in the Sea-to-Sky corridor like Squamish and Whistler, to allow up to three, four, or even six housing units on lots previously zoned strictly for single-family or duplex homes. For homeowners and investors, this means you can legally build an Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU), laneway house, or multiplex on your property without requiring a lengthy, expensive rezoning process.

### How much does an ADU cost in British Columbia?
The cost to build a detached Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) or laneway home in the Sea-to-Sky corridor typically ranges between $350 to $500+ per square foot. For a standard 800-square-foot ADU in Squamish or West Vancouver, total project costs generally fall between $280,000 and $400,000. This includes site prep, utilities, permits, high-performance Step Code compliance, and finishes. Costs fluctuate based on site access, slope conditions, and whether bedrock blasting is required.

### Why build an ADU in the Sea-to-Sky Corridor?
Building an ADU provides a massive ROI through passive rental income and increased property valuation. With the housing shortage across Squamish, Whistler, and North Vancouver, rental demand is at an all-time high. A well-built ADU can generate $2,500 to $4,000+ per month in rental income. Furthermore, it creates flexible living arrangements for aging parents or adult children.
