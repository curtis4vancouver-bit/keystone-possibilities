## 🧬 Part 1: Core Research & Fact Check

### 1. The Core Event
The FDA has officially scheduled PCAC hearings on **July 23–24, 2026** to evaluate several bulk drug substances for inclusion on the compounding lists (503A/503B).
- **July 23 Agenda**: BPC-157, KPV, TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4), and MOTS-c.
- **July 24 Agenda**: Epitalon, Semax, and Emideltide.

### 2. Category 1 vs. Category 2 (Bulks List)
- **Category 2 (Current Status)**: Restricted. These are substances nominated for compounding but flagged by the FDA for significant safety concerns or lack of clinical safety data. Compounding pharmacies cannot legally compound them.
- **Category 1 (The Goal)**: Approved for Compounding. If moved to Category 1, licensed compounding pharmacies can legally prepare these peptides when prescribed by a physician for individual patients.

### 3. The "Compounding" Timeline Context (Series Continuity)
- **Where we left off**: We previously discussed the **Zinc-Copper Reset** when running growth hormone secretagogues like CJC-1295.

### 4. Key Clinical Facts (The Science)
- **BPC-157**: Synthetic pentadecapeptide derived from human gastric juice. Promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation) and collagen synthesis to heal tendons/ligaments.
- **TB-500**: Synthetic version of the active domain of Thymosin Beta-4. Promotes actin sequestration, cell migration, and tissue repair systemically.
- **MOTS-c**: Mitochondrial-derived peptide. Regulates metabolic homeostasis and acts as an "exercise mimetic" by activating the AMPK pathway (via folate-methionine cycle inhibition).
