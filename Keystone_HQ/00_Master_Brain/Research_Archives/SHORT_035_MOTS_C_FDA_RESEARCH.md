    *   **July 23 Agenda**: BPC-157, KPV, TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4), and MOTS-c.
    *   **July 24 Agenda**: Epitalon, Semax, and Emideltide.

### 2. Category 1 vs. Category 2 (The Bulks List)
*   **Category 1 (The Goal)**: Approved for Compounding. Substances placed in Category 1 can be legally compounded by 503A (individual prescription) and 503B (outsourcing facilities) pharmacies when prescribed by a physician.
*   **Category 2 (The Threat)**: Restricted/Banned. Substances flagged for safety concerns or lacking clinical trials. Compounding pharmacies are legally banned from producing them, driving patients to the unregulated gray market.

### 3. Key Clinical/Biochemical Facts for the Video
*   **BPC-157**: Synthetic pentadecapeptide that mimics gastric juice compounds. Triggers angiogenesis (blood vessel formation) and collagen synthesis. Framed as "structural framing repair" for tissues.
*   **TB-500**: Synthetic Thymosin Beta-4 active domain. Promotes cell migration and actin sequestration. Framed as the "site superintendent" directing healing signals systemically.
*   **MOTS-c**: Mitochondrial-derived peptide that mimics exercise by activating the AMPK pathway. Promotes metabolic efficiency and protects muscle tissue from wasting.
