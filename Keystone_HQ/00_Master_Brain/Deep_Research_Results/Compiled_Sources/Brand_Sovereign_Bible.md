<!-- CONTEXT: Brand Voice / 1 · Universal Voice Rules -->
## 1 · Universal Voice Rules

These rules apply to **every** piece of content across all Keystone brands — scripts, blogs, captions, thumbnails, descriptions, and internal documentation.

<!-- CONTEXT: Brand Voice / Tone -->
### Tone

| Attribute       | Do                                           | Don't                                       |
|-----------------|----------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Authority       | Speak from lived experience and data         | Lecture or gatekeep                          |
| Approachability | Write like you're explaining to a friend     | Use jargon without explanation               |
| Confidence      | Make clear, direct statements                | Hedge with "maybe," "sort of," "kind of"    |
| Warmth          | Show you care about the audience's outcome   | Be robotic or detached                       |

<!-- CONTEXT: Brand Voice / Style -->
### Style

- **Short, punchy sentences.** If a sentence runs past two lines — break it.
- **Canadian English** at all times (`colour`, `favourite`, `centre`, `licence`).
- **Active voice.** "We build additions" not "Additions are built by us."
- **Never corporate.** No "leveraging synergies," no "solutions-oriented approach," no buzzwords.
- **Numbers talk.** Use specific data when available ("40% CTR improvement," "3-week build").
- **Contractions are fine.** Write `it's`, `we've`, `you'll` — it sounds human.
- **Oxford comma.** Always.

<!-- CONTEXT: Brand Voice / Banned Phrases -->
### Banned Phrases

- "In today's fast-paced world…"
- "Without further ado…"
- "Game-changer" (unless quoting someone)
- "Synergy" / "Leverage" / "Disrupt"
- "Content creator" when referring to Wayne — he's a **builder**, **coach**, or **producer**
- "AI-generated" in any public-facing content
- "Hey guys" — use "Hey folks" or no greeting

<!-- CONTEXT: Brand Voice / Formatting Conventions -->
### Formatting Conventions

- **Em dashes** (`—`) for interjections, not hyphens or en dashes.
- **Ellipses** sparingly — max one per paragraph.
- **Bold** for emphasis in written content. ALL CAPS only in thumbnails.
- **Hashtags:** lowercase, no underscores (`#keystonepossibilities` not `#Keystone_Possibilities`).

## 3 · Protocol / Recomposition Voice

**Brand persona:** The no-BS performance coach who reads the studies so you don't have to.

<!-- CONTEXT: Brand Voice / Core Attributes -->
### Core Attributes

- **Science-backed but not academic.** Cite studies, but translate them. No one reads an abstract in the gym.
- **Coach tone.** Direct, motivational, zero fluff. Think personal trainer who also reads PubMed.
- **Real results focus.** Wayne's personal journey is the proof — use it.
- **Dash-syllable format** for medical/pharmaceutical terms to aid pronunciation:
  - Semaglutide → **Sem-a-glue-tide**
  - Testosterone → **Tes-tos-ter-one**
  - Tirzepatide → **Tir-zep-a-tide**
  - Metformin → **Met-for-min**
  - Rapamycin → **Ra-pa-my-sin**

<!-- CONTEXT: Brand Voice / Vocabulary -->
### Vocabulary

- **Say:** stack, protocol, bloodwork, results, dose, cycle
- **Don't say:** regimen (too clinical), journey (too soft), hack (too bro-science)
- **Say:** performance, longevity, optimization
- **Don't say:** anti-aging (negative framing), biohacking (overused)

- "This is my personal experience — not medical advice."

## 5 · Cross-Brand Interaction Rules

When content spans multiple brands (e.g., Protocol video with Recomposition Music background):

1. **Lead with the primary brand's voice.** The music is background — it doesn't change the Protocol tone.
2. **Credit the music brand** in the description: "Background: Recomposition Music — 432 Hz Focus"
3. **Never mix Possibilities and Protocol** in the same piece of content. They serve different audiences.
4. **Cross-linking is encouraged** but must feel natural, not forced.

- Wastes prompt space on information the model already has
