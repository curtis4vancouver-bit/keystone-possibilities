What if forty-five percent of the weight you're losing on Ozempic isn't fat?

That's what the clinical data says. And if you work with your body for a living, nobody is talking to you about it. Until now.

---

Welcome to Keystone Recomposition. My name is Wayne. I'm forty-three years old. I've spent over twenty years in active construction. Framing houses. Running job sites. Carrying timber up mountain roads in Squamish, British Columbia.

I'm not a doctor. I'm not a biohacker influencer. I'm a builder. And my body is the most important tool I own. This channel documents exactly what I'm doing to rebuild it. With published science. Personal data. And zero bullshit.

Let's get into it.

---

Fifteen million Americans are currently taking GLP-1 medications. Ozempic. Mounjaro. Wegovy. Zepbound. The weight is falling off. And for most people, that feels like a miracle.

But here's what the published clinical trials actually say about what you're losing.

The landmark STEP-1 trial for semaglutide — that's Ozempic — reported an average weight loss of fifteen point three kilograms. But six point nine two kilograms of that was lean body mass. That is forty-five percent. Nearly half of what those patients lost was not fat.

In normal dieting, there's a well-established rule called the quarter fat-free mass rule. It says that roughly twenty-five percent of weight loss should come from lean tissue. GLP-1 drugs shatter that rule.

The SURMOUNT-1 trial for tirzepatide — that's Mounjaro — confirmed approximately twenty-five percent lean mass loss over seventy-two weeks. And a separate network meta-analysis in the International Journal of Obesity found that lean mass loss ranged from twenty-five to forty percent across multiple GLP-1 drugs.

Now — and this is important. A researcher at UC Davis named Keith Baar published commentary showing that much of the lean mass loss on DEXA scans isn't actually skeletal muscle. A significant portion is liver volume shrinkage and organ mass reduction. Your liver gets smaller because it's dumping stored fat. That shows up as lean mass loss on the scan.

But even after accounting for that, the skeletal muscle loss is real. And if you're over forty, you're already in a biological war against sarcopenia. The natural age-related muscle wasting that strips three to eight percent of your lean tissue every single decade.

---

Here's why this hits different if you work with your body.

A software developer loses ten pounds of muscle. He doesn't notice at his desk. A builder loses ten pounds of muscle. He can't lift the header into place. He can't swing a framing hammer for eight hours. His back gives out carrying sheets of plywood up a set of scaffolding.

Muscle isn't vanity for us. It's infrastructure. It's the load-bearing beam of the building that is your body. And if you strip that beam while the rest of the structure stays standing, eventually something fails.

---

So how do we stop the leak. The protocol has four steps. And none of them are complicated. But all of them are non-negotiable.

Step one is your protein floor. This is the foundation. The Harvard Science Review published data stating that GLP-1 users need a minimum of one point two to one point five grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily just to maintain nitrogen balance. Just to break even.

For a two-hundred-pound active builder, that means a baseline of roughly one hundred eighty to two hundred grams of protein every single day. When your appetite is crushed by the medication and you can barely look at food, hitting this number is the hardest thing you'll do.

But think of it this way. Your body is a construction site. Protein is the raw material. The concrete, the lumber, the rebar. You can signal your body to build all day long. But if there's no material on site, nothing gets built.

---

Step two is resistance training. And I don't mean going for a jog. I don't mean a thirty-minute walk. I mean heavy compound lifts with progressive overload.

The American College of Sports Medicine published their updated position paper confirming that heavy resistance training is the single most effective way to activate the Akt-mTOR pathway. This is the cellular mechanism that tells your body preserve muscle, burn fat. Without this signal, your body will digest your muscle tissue for energy. It doesn't know the difference between a famine and a GLP-1 prescription.

Three to four sessions per week. Compound movements. Squats, deadlifts, rows, overhead press. Heavy enough that the last two reps feel like they might not happen. That's the signal. That's the switch.

---

Step three is creatine monohydrate. Five grams. Every single day. No cycling. No loading phase. Just five grams with water.

This is not a bodybuilder supplement. This is one of the most clinically validated muscle protectants in medical history. A meta-analysis in the Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition proved that creatine during a caloric deficit significantly increases lean mass retention and intracellular hydration.

On a GLP-1, where your food intake is severely restricted, creatine acts as an armor plate. Drawing water into your muscle cells and keeping them metabolically active even when calories are low.

---

Step four is the one that almost no one talks about. The tissue scaffolding.

Rapid fat loss strips volume from your face. That's the phenomenon everyone calls Ozempic face. Simultaneously, severe calorie deficits can force hair follicles into a resting phase, accelerating shedding.

This is where published research on copper peptides, specifically GHK-Cu, becomes relevant. Clinical data in the International Journal of Molecular Sciences shows that GHK-Cu stimulates collagen and elastin synthesis by up to seventy percent and increases basic fibroblast growth factor by two hundred and thirty percent.

Now I want to be very clear. This is published research I'm reviewing, not a prescription. Peptides are a rapidly evolving area of science and I am not qualified to tell you what to take. But the data on tissue remodeling is compelling enough that it's part of my personal research.

---

This is not medical advice. Always consult your physician before starting, stopping, or modifying any medication or supplement protocol.

---

Let me share what implementing this protocol has looked like in my own documentation.

Within the first thirty days of running the full stack — two hundred grams of protein, five grams of creatine, four structured heavy lift sessions per week, and a personal GHK-Cu research protocol — my documented shoulder and back recovery compressed from five days down to forty-eight hours. The joint stiffness that used to greet me every single morning on the job site. Gone.

This is not about one magic pill. A solid house is not held up by one nail. It's protein, resistance training, intracellular hydration, and tissue scaffolding. Working together as one structural system.
