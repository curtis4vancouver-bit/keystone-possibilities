# Recomposition Music — Brand Identity

> **Brand:** Recomposition Music
> **Owner:** Wayne Stevenson
> **Genre:** Ambient, healing frequencies, meditation, focus music

To create ambient soundscapes and healing frequency compositions that support focus, meditation, recovery, and rest — serving as both standalone art and the sonic foundation of the Protocol/Recomposition ecosystem.

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / 4 · Content Pillars -->
## 4 · Content Pillars

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Pillar 1: Focus Frequencies -->
### Pillar 1: Focus Frequencies
- 432 Hz compositions designed for deep work and concentration
- Minimum 60-minute tracks (Spotify algorithm preference)
- Binaural beat integration where appropriate
- No sudden changes — steady [[STATE|state]] throughout

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Pillar 2: Recovery Ambient -->
### Pillar 2: Recovery Ambient
- Tracks designed for post-workout or pre-sleep
- Lower BPM, deeper tones, spacious reverb
- Paired with specific Protocol video episodes
- Designed to slow heart rate and promote parasympathetic activation

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Pillar 3: Protocol Backgrounds -->
### Pillar 3: Protocol Backgrounds
- Custom tracks created specifically for Protocol YouTube videos
- Subtle enough to sit behind voice-over without competing
- Mood-matched to video content (intense for stacking episodes, calm for results)
- Published separately on Spotify with "As heard in The Wolverine Stack" tags

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Pillar 4: Meditation & Stillness -->
### Pillar 4: Meditation & Stillness
- Pure ambient compositions — no beats, no rhythm
- Nature-infused soundscapes (water, wind, forest)
- Extended play formats (1-4 hours for YouTube, 60+ min for Spotify)
- Tied to wellness themes without being "new age"

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / 6 · Musical Direction -->
## 6 · Musical Direction

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Sound Characteristics -->
### Sound Characteristics

- **Tempo:** 50-80 BPM for recovery, 80-120 BPM for focus (no faster)
- **Key frequencies:** 432 Hz tuning preferred; 528 Hz for specific healing tracks
- **Texture:** Layered pads, soft drones, granular synthesis, field recordings
- **Dynamics:** Minimal — no sharp transients, no sudden volume changes
- **Length:** Minimum 60 minutes for Spotify; 1-4 hours for YouTube ambient videos
- **Transitions:** Seamless — listeners should not notice track boundaries in playlists

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Production Standards -->
### Production Standards

- Master loudness: -14 LUFS (Spotify normalization target)
- No clipping, no harsh frequencies above 12 kHz
- Stereo width: wide but mono-compatible
- Format: WAV 24-bit/48kHz master, distribution via standard aggregator

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / What This Brand is NOT -->
### What This Brand is NOT

- Not EDM, not lo-fi hip hop, not beats-to-study-to
- Not new age chanting or guided meditation
- Not podcast background music
- Not royalty-free stock music

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / 7 · Platform Strategy -->
## 7 · Platform Strategy

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Spotify (Primary) -->
### Spotify (Primary)

- **Release cadence:** 2 tracks per month minimum
- **Playlist strategy:**
  - "Recomposition Focus" — 432 Hz deep work tracks
  - "Recomposition Recovery" — post-workout and pre-sleep
  - "Recomposition Stillness" — pure ambient meditation
- **Track titles:** Descriptive, minimal (e.g., "Deep Focus 432 · I," "Recovery Ambient · III")
- **Album art:** Purple-to-teal gradient, abstract waveform, no faces (see `../BRAND_VISUAL.md`)

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / YouTube (Secondary) -->
### YouTube (Secondary)

- **Format:** Ambient video — album art or abstract visuals with waveform overlay
- **Duration:** 1-4 hours for background play
- **Description:** Minimal — track name, frequency, purpose, link to Spotify
- **No hard CTAs** — the music brand doesn't "sell"

<!-- CONTEXT: Identity / Protocol Integration -->
### Protocol Integration

- Every Protocol YouTube video uses Recomposition Music as background
- Description credits: "Background: Recomposition Music — [Track Name] · [Frequency]"
- Spotify link included in Protocol video descriptions
