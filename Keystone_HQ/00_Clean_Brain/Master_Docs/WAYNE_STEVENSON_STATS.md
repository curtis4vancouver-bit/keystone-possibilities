## 🧬 Crucial Mineral Interaction: The Zinc-Copper Connection
> [!IMPORTANT]
> A critical biological feedback loop has been identified when combining CJC-1295 (no DAC) and GHK-Cu. Active supplementation and cycling are required to prevent toxicity and growth hormone resistance.

### 1. Pituitary Zinc Depletion
Growth hormone is stored in secretory granules within the anterior pituitary gland in the form of **zinc-bound crystals**. Regular pulsatile growth hormone release stimulated by CJC-1295 (no DAC) and Ipamorelin depletes these pituitary zinc stores over time.

### 2. Growth Hormone Resistance & IGF-1 Stalls
When pituitary zinc is depleted:
*   The liver develops relative resistance to growth hormone.
*   Production of Insulin-like Growth Factor 1 (IGF-1) stalls, blunting the anabolic benefits of the peptide cycle.

### 3. Copper Antagonism
Exogenous copper introduced via GHK-Cu injections is a direct mineral antagonist to zinc. The combination of high copper intake and pituitary zinc depletion creates a metabolic imbalance, leading to **relative copper toxicity** and severe zinc deficiency.

### 4. Recalibration Protocol
*   **Intra-cycle Intervention**: Supplement with **15 to 50 mg of elemental zinc daily** during active peptide cycles containing CJC-1295 and GHK-Cu.
*   **Post-cycle Intervention**: When cycling off CJC-1295 and GHK-Cu (e.g., at the end of a 5-month cycle), execute a **post-cycle zinc reset** (supplementing zinc without copper) to completely restore zinc status, clear excess copper, and desensitize pituitary receptors.
