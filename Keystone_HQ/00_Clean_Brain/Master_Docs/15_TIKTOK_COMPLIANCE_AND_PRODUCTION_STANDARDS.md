### 1. The Four Compliance Axioms
1.  **Strict Academic Framing:** All peptide discussions must be presented as **clinical research overviews or individual academic case studies**. Never speak in the second person ("you should take this") or give direct advice. Speak strictly in the third person or as personal diary logs.
2.  **No Purchase Promotions:** Never mention suppliers, display pricing, show product packaging, or include affiliate links to chemical vendors in bios or descriptions.
3.  **No Dosing/Administration Guides:** Never show syringes, needles, or the act of administration. Refer to dosing as "titration schedules" and injections as "administration methods."
4.  **No Weight Loss Claims:** Avoid words like "lose fat fast", "appetite suppressant", or "weight loss injection". Use compliant scientific terms like "lipid oxidation", "metabolic recomposition", or "microbiome barrier integrity."

### 1. Screen Layout & Presentation Rules
*   **Full-Screen Alternating Standard:** All clips are full-screen (9:16 or 16:9). No picture-in-picture borders.
*   **The 1-Second Pan-Off Jump-Cut Prevention Rule:** For every talking-head avatar segment, the video must start with Wayne talking facing the camera. In the **last 1 second**, the camera must pan away or pan off-camera to a desaturated background (or camera-shake motion). When the video transitions to the next talking clip, Wayne can be shown talking from a slightly different framing, completely bypassing the awkward facial jump-cuts common in continuous AI generation.
*   **Unified Voiceover Consistency:** For all B-roll segments, the prompt must still contain the script dialogue text so the avatar voice generator compiles the voiceover consistently throughout all clips, maintaining a fluid, unified voice.
*   **10-Second Prompt Segmentation:** Every script (whether 1 minute, 4 minutes, or 20 minutes) must be programmatically broken down into strict, contiguous 10-second segments. Each segment has its own precise dialogue segment and visual rendering prompt.
*   **Persistent Disclaimer Card:** A prominent medical disclaimer card must appear on screen during the first 20 seconds of the video. Additionally, a small, readable scrolling ticker must be present at the bottom throughout the entire video.
    *   *Disclaimer Text:* `"STRICTLY EDUCATIONAL CASE STUDY. NOT MEDICAL ADVICE. INDIVIDUAL OBSERVATIONS FOR SCHOLARLY PURPOSES. CONSULT A LICENSED PHYSICIAN."`
