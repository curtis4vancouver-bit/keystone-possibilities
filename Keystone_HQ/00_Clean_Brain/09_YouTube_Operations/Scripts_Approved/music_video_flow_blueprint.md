2. **The 9-Second Whip-Pan Rule:** Every single clip MUST execute a rapid 1-second camera whip-pan (left, right, or vertical tilt) starting exactly at second 9 to absorb the cut boundary into a clean motion blur transition.
3. **No Empty Machinery Rule:** Always describe B-roll using rich, natural textures (timber joints, glass glints, ocean drone sweeps, concrete texture) to guarantee flawless photorealism and avoid bad-looking or ghostly automated objects.
