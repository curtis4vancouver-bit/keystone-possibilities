The Angiogenic Paradox (Theoretical Risks)

The most significant safety concern regarding BPC-157 and Thymosin Beta-4 is their relationship with tumor growth. Under Judah Folkman's Concept of Tumor Angiogenesis, no tumor can grow beyond 1-2 millimeters in diameter without first recruiting its own blood supply. Therefore, any substance that aggressively upregulates angiogenesis could theoretically nourish existing, undiagnosed cancers.

However, the scientific reality for BPC-157 appears to be more nuanced. In multiple animal cancer studies, BPC-157 has actually demonstrated anti-tumor effects, such as reducing lung metastases from melanoma and counteracting tumor cachexia (muscle wasting) in colon adenocarcinoma by downregulating inflammatory cytokines like IL-6 and TNF-α.
