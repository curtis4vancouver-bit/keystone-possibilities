Navigating BC Hydro's Underground Mandate

Since September 2024, BC Hydro has enforced a strict safety and compliance regulation: **all civil underground electrical installations** on public property and private parcels must be completed by a recognized, Registered Civil Contractor. General contractors and builders who are not on this list are legally prohibited from touching primary or secondary service conduits, duct banks, and connection vaults.

Keystone Possibilities Ltd is officially certified and registered under BC Hydro's civil supplier framework for the Lower Mainland & Fraser Valley. Backed by twenty years of corridor construction experience, including major Sea-to-Sky Highway bridge upgrades with Peter Kiewit Sons, our team provides seamless civil execution from bedrock trenching to final electrical grid tie-in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I need a Registered Civil Contractor for BC Hydro?

Since September 2024, BC Hydro mandates that all civil underground electrical installations (such as trenching, placing conduits, and setting concrete vaults) on both public and private property must be performed by a contractor listed on their Registered Civil Contractor List. Unregistered builders cannot legally perform this scope.

What civil utility services can Keystone Possibilities handle directly?

Keystone handles the entire civil package: site excavation, utility trenching, conduit placement, vault installations, primary/secondary service connections, and structural coordination. By handling this in-house, we eliminate subcontractor scheduling delays.

How does BC Hydro's capital plan affect infrastructure in Squamish?

BC Hydro is investing $36 billion in grid modernization, with over $500 million allocated directly to the Sea-to-Sky and North Shore. This includes new substations in Squamish, transmission upgrades, and the 19km Woodfibre LNG interconnection starting in 2028, significantly straining the local supply of registered contractors.

How do I verify a contractor's registration with BC Hydro?

You can verify active credentials directly on BC Hydro's official Registered Civil Contractor List website under the Lower Mainland & Fraser Valley region, where Keystone Possibilities Ltd is listed, backed by our COR safety certification and WorkSafeBC clearance.

What is the fee structure for Keystone's civil project management?

We operate on a transparent flat-fee project management model (typically 10% to 15% depending on project scale). Because we do not mark up trade costs or materials, our fiduciary commitment is entirely focused on negotiating the best prices and ensuring strict engineering compliance.
