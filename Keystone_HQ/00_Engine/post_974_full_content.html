Most Mounjaro success stories come from people who work desk jobs. They meal-prep on Sunday, hit the gym at 6am, and track every calorie.

I pour concrete. I frame houses. I run a full construction crew in one of the most physically demanding environments you can imagine — the Sea-to-Sky Corridor in British Columbia.

And I still lost 38 lbs in 26 weeks on Mounjaro.

This is my complete protocol. Three phases. No BS. Just what actually worked for a 40-year-old man who can't take a lunch break whenever he wants.

Why Mounjaro (Tirzepatide) Works Differently Than Everything Else

I've tried cutting before. I've tried intermittent fasting on job sites. I've tried high-protein diets while swinging a hammer for 10 hours.

The hunger always won.

Mounjaro (the brand name for Tirzepatide) is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. In plain English, it targets two hunger-suppressing pathways simultaneously — something semaglutide-based medications like Ozempic and Wegovy only partially address. The result for me was simple: for the first time in years, I was actually able to eat the way I knew I should eat, because the medication removed the constant drive to overeat.

That's the single biggest thing nobody tells you. Mounjaro doesn't do the work for you. It removes the biological noise that was making the work impossible.

My 3-Phase 26-Week Protocol

I broke the 26 weeks into three distinct phases. Each one had a different goal, a different relationship with food, and a different physical output.

Phase 1 (Weeks 1–8): Adaptation & Dose Titration

The first eight weeks were about getting my body used to the medication and finding my minimum effective dose.

Starting dose: 2.5mg weekly injection
End of phase dose: 5mg weekly injection

What I ate: I didn't try to be perfect. I focused on one rule: high protein, every meal. On a construction site, that meant canned tuna, Greek yogurt, hardboiled eggs, and a lot of protein shakes mixed in my truck. I wasn't tracking macros obsessively — I was just eliminating the carb-heavy, processed garbage that was easy to grab on a busy job site.

The result: I dropped approximately 14 lbs in Phase 1. Most of this was water weight and the rapid deflation that comes with dramatically reducing processed carbohydrates and sugar. My energy on site was actually better within two weeks, which surprised me.

Key lesson: The side effects (nausea, fatigue) are worst in the first 2–3 weeks. They pass. Don't quit during the adaptation window.

Phase 2 (Weeks 9–18): The Recomposition Phase

This is where the real work happened. I stabilized my dose at 7.5mg and shifted my focus from pure weight loss to body recomposition — losing fat while preserving or building muscle mass.

The muscle preservation protocol: This is the part most Mounjaro users miss. GLP-1 medications suppress appetite so effectively that many people don't eat enough protein to preserve lean muscle mass. You can end up 40 lbs lighter but look "skinny fat" because you lost as much muscle as fat.

To combat this:

  • I hit 180–200g of protein per day, even on days I wasn't hungry
  • I continued heavy physical labour on job sites (which acted as natural resistance training)
  • I added 3x per week resistance training sessions on evenings — nothing fancy, just compound lifts (squat, deadlift, press, row)
  • I added BPC-157 (a peptide) to support joint recovery, because construction work combined with resistance training creates significant joint stress

What I ate: By Phase 2, my appetite was dramatically reduced. The challenge shifted from "not eating too much" to "eating enough of the right things." I built every meal around a protein anchor: chicken thighs, ground beef, eggs, cottage cheese, or a protein shake. Everything else was built around that.

The result: I dropped another 18 lbs and visibly changed my body composition. My arms and shoulders got noticeably more defined even as the scale dropped. This is what recomposition looks like when you protect the muscle.

Phase 3 (Weeks 19–26): Consolidation & Lifestyle Integration

The final phase was about turning this into a permanent lifestyle rather than a temporary protocol.

Dose: Maintained at 7.5mg–10mg depending on how my body responded week to week.

The mental shift: By week 19, I had lost most of the weight I was targeting. The new challenge was psychological: learning to eat intuitively at a new, lower setpoint without the medication doing all the heavy lifting. I started extending the time between doses slightly (under medical supervision) to test my body's own hunger regulation.

What changed on the job site: The physical difference on the construction site was dramatic. Carrying 38 fewer pounds while climbing scaffolding, framing walls, and moving material is not a subtle change. My knees felt better. My back felt better. I had more endurance at the end of a 10-hour day than I had in my mid-30s.

The result: Final 6 lbs dropped, and — more importantly — the habits were now automatic. I didn't need to white-knuckle the diet anymore.

The Total Numbers

What I Would Do Differently

1. Start resistance training in Week 1, not Week 9.
I waited too long. Even two sessions a week from the very beginning would have preserved more muscle mass in Phase 1.

2. Get bloodwork done before you start.
I wish I had baseline testosterone, HbA1c, and full metabolic panel numbers before I started so I could see the full before/after picture beyond just the scale.

3. Don't skip the protein on low-appetite days.
The days when Mounjaro kills your hunger completely are the days you think "I'll just skip lunch." Don't. Force a protein shake. Protect the muscle.

For Men Over 40 Specifically

If you are over 40, running a business or working physically demanding work, and you've tried everything else — this works differently. It's not a willpower issue. It never was. GLP-1 medications level the playing field between your biology and your intentions.

The key is doing it strategically:

  • Protect your muscle mass aggressively with protein and resistance training
  • Work with a doctor who understands body recomposition, not just weight loss
  • Track your composition, not just your weight
