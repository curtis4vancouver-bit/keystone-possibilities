<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why Mounjaro (Tirzepatide) Works Differently Than Everything Else</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I've tried cutting before. I've tried intermittent fasting on job sites. I've tried high-protein diets while swinging a hammer for 10 hours.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The hunger always won.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Mounjaro (the brand name for Tirzepatide) is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. In plain English, it targets&nbsp;<em>two</em>&nbsp;hunger-suppressing pathways simultaneously — something semaglutide-based medications like Ozempic and Wegovy only partially address. The result for me was simple: for the first time in years, I was actually able to eat the way I knew I&nbsp;<em>should</em>&nbsp;eat, because the medication removed the constant drive to overeat.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That's the single biggest thing nobody tells you. Mounjaro doesn't do the work for you. It removes the biological noise that was making the work impossible.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">My 3-Phase 26-Week Protocol</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I broke the 26 weeks into three distinct phases. Each one had a different goal, a different relationship with food, and a different physical output.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Phase 1 (Weeks 1–8): Adaptation &amp; Dose Titration</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The first eight weeks were about getting my body used to the medication and finding my minimum effective dose.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Starting dose:</strong>&nbsp;2.5mg weekly injection<br><strong>End of phase dose:</strong>&nbsp;5mg weekly injection</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>What I ate:</strong>&nbsp;I didn't try to be perfect. I focused on one rule:&nbsp;<strong>high protein, every meal</strong>. On a construction site, that meant canned tuna, Greek yogurt, hardboiled eggs, and a lot of protein shakes mixed in my truck. I wasn't tracking macros obsessively — I was just eliminating the carb-heavy, processed garbage that was easy to grab on a busy job site.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>The result:</strong>&nbsp;I dropped approximately&nbsp;<strong>14 lbs</strong>&nbsp;in Phase 1. Most of this was water weight and the rapid deflation that comes with dramatically reducing processed carbohydrates and sugar. My energy on site was actually&nbsp;<em>better</em>&nbsp;within two weeks, which surprised me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Key lesson:</strong>&nbsp;The side effects (nausea, fatigue) are worst in the first 2–3 weeks. They pass. Don't quit during the adaptation window.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Phase 2 (Weeks 9–18): The Recomposition Phase</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This is where the real work happened. I stabilized my dose at&nbsp;<strong>7.5mg</strong>&nbsp;and shifted my focus from pure weight loss to body recomposition — losing fat while preserving or building muscle mass.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>The muscle preservation protocol:</strong>&nbsp;This is the part most Mounjaro users miss. GLP-1 medications suppress appetite so effectively that many people don't eat&nbsp;<em>enough protein</em>&nbsp;to preserve lean muscle mass. You can end up 40 lbs lighter but look "skinny fat" because you lost as much muscle as fat.</p>
