<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What the FDA Actually Did (And Didn't Do)</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>On April 22, 2026, the FDA officially removed 12 peptides from the Category 2 bulk drug substances list. This came after <strong>Robert F. Kennedy Jr.</strong> announced the proposed removal during a podcast with Joe Rogan in February 2026, and then confirmed it publicly on April 15th.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The 12 peptides removed include some of the most widely discussed compounds in the biohacking and regenerative medicine space:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>BPC-157</strong> (Body Protection Compound)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>TB-500</strong> (Thymosin Beta-4)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>GHK-Cu</strong> (Copper Peptide)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>And 9 others previously placed under restriction</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But here's the critical distinction that <strong>almost every headline gets wrong:</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Off Category 2 ≠ On Category 1.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>These peptides are no longer on the restricted list. But they are NOT on the approved list either. They exist in a regulatory gray zone.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why This Matters: The Gray Zone</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When a peptide is removed from Category 2, it means compounding pharmacies are <em>technically</em> no longer restricted from handling it. But without Category 1 designation, there's no standardized regulatory framework for purity, dosing, or quality control.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This creates a gray market situation. Independent testing has found contamination rates as high as <strong>30%</strong> in peptides purchased from unregulated sources. That's not a typo — nearly one-third of tested samples contained impurities, incorrect dosing, or undisclosed compounds.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The July 2026 PCAC Hearing</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The <strong>Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC)</strong> is scheduled to convene on <strong>July 23-24, 2026</strong> to evaluate whether these 12 peptides should be placed on Category 1 — the approved list for compounding.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This is the real milestone. Category 1 placement would mean:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Licensed compounding pharmacies can legally produce and distribute these peptides</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Quality and purity standards would be established</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Physicians could prescribe with regulatory backing</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Insurance coverage becomes possible (eventually)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
