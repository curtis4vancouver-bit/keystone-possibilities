What Is the Wolverine Stack?

The Wolverine Stack combines two peptides that target different layers of tissue repair:

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound 157) is a synthetic 15-amino-acid peptide derived from a protective protein found naturally in human gastric juice. In animal studies, it has demonstrated the ability to accelerate tendon, ligament, muscle, and gut lining repair. Key mechanisms include angiogenesis (the formation of new blood vessels), nitric oxide modulation, and growth factor signaling.

TB-500 is a synthetic peptide derived from the active region of Thymosin Beta-4, a naturally occurring 43-amino-acid protein in the human body. TB-500 specifically contains the 7-amino-acid actin-binding domain that promotes cell migration and reduces inflammation.

In construction terms: BPC-157 is the plumber — running new supply lines to damaged areas. TB-500 is the electrician — directing the repair crew to the exact location of the problem.

The FDA Category 2 Ruling — What Actually Happened

In September 2023, the FDA moved BPC-157, TB-500, and over a dozen other peptides to the Category 2 list under Section 503A of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act. This meant compounding pharmacies could no longer produce these compounds for patients.

Category 2 does not mean "banned." It means the FDA determined there is insufficient human clinical trial data to confirm safety for bulk compounding. The distinction matters.

A coalition of physicians and compounding pharmacies are actively fighting to restore BPC-157 to Category 1. The FDA has scheduled the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) to meet on July 23, 2026 to review BPC-157 and TB-500 for potential reinstatement.

In Canada, the regulatory environment is different but the result is similar — BPC-157 is not an approved drug for human use under Health Canada, meaning physicians cannot legally prescribe it.
